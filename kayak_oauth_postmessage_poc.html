KAYAK OAuth postMessage Account Fixation

Cross-origin token injection via missing event.origin validation — systemic across Google & Naver OAuth
Root Cause: KAYAK's OAuth postMessage listeners check event.data.action === "googleLogin" (or "naverLogin") but do NOT check event.origin. Any cross-origin page can inject an attacker-controlled access_token. The Microsoft/Outlook handler in the same codebase correctly validates origin.
Google (kayak.com)
Naver (kayak.co.kr)

0 Establish victim identity

Log into KAYAK with your own Google account. Note your name/email on kayak.com/account.

1 Get attacker's Google access_token

How to capture the token (video-friendly)

  1. Open a different browser (or incognito with a separate Google account)
  2. Go to kayak.com/login
  3. Open DevTools → Network tab → filter: googleauth
  4. Click "Sign in with Google" and complete the OAuth flow
  5. Find the request: POST /k/run/googleauth/login
  6. In the Payload tab, copy the accessToken value

2 Launch attack

Opens kayak.com/login in a popup and injects the attacker's token via cross-origin postMessage.

After clicking Launch: click "Sign in with Google" on the popup.

Idle

3 Verify account takeover

Navigate to kayak.com/account — the account is now the attacker's. Your name, email, and saved data have changed.
