2

Clicking the button below will:

1. Open kayak.com/login in a popup
2. Immediately start sending a different user's Google token into that page via postMessage
3. When you click "Sign in with Google" on the popup, KAYAK processes the injected token instead of yours
After clicking the button below: Click "Sign in with Google" on the KAYAK login popup that appears. That's the only action needed.

What happened

This page — hosted on — sent a postMessage to kayak.com/login containing a different user's Google OAuth token.

KAYAK's login handler checked event.data.action === "googleLogin" but did not check event.origin. It accepted the token from this cross-origin page and logged you into the other user's account.

An attacker could use this to log victims into the attacker's KAYAK account. Any credit cards, passport numbers, or bookings the victim enters are then accessible to the attacker.
